The Four Guiding Principles

These four principles govern every call in this queue. You'll use them for the rest of this program and in every live case after May 22.

Click "Show common misread" on each card to see where specialists get tripped up.

Principle 1

Neutrality

Thumbtack is neutral. If the customer and pro interacted, we do not arbitrate what happened or didn't happen, or the truthfulness of the review.

Common misread

"The pro's story is detailed and convincing, so the review must be wrong."

It's tempting. But a detailed story is not a policy violation. We need a violation, not a stronger narrative.

Principle 2

No edits

We either remove the review or leave it. We do not edit reviews.

Common misread

"Just take out the bad part and leave the rest."

There's no middle path. The review stays as written or it comes down. We don't edit.

Principle 3

Clear violation only

Thumbtack's stance is not to remove reviews unless a clear violation of the Content and Review Policy is confirmed.

Common misread

"This review feels off — let's remove it to be safe."

The default is always to leave the review. If you can't point to a clear violation, it stays.

Principle 4

Refunded leads don't auto-disqualify

If a lead is refunded, it does not automatically disqualify a review. Reviews are judged solely on the interaction between the customer and the pro.

Common misread

"The customer got their money back, so this review shouldn't count."

The financial outcome is a separate question. We judge the interaction between the customer and the pro — not who won the chargeback.

Meet Marcus.
